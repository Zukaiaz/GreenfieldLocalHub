# Every website has to follow computing legislation. For GLH, when a customer creates an account or orders produce, their personal data will be collected. This is why the system must comply with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018 (DPA). These laws ensure that personal data, such as names, emails, addresses, and phone numbers, is collected and stored securely with the user’s consent. Consent will be gained through cookies, which require users to accept or deny them before any data is collected. Cookies are small files placed on a user’s device by their browser, used for tracking, authentication, and personalization. Users will also be able to view, edit, or delete their data, and a privacy policy page will explain user rights, data retention policies, and how customers like Lizzie and Joel can manage their personal information. Passwords will be hashed automatically as Visual Studio MVC provides built-in encryption, keeping accounts secure. This ensures that account creation, login, and checkout features comply with data protection law and protect user privacy.

# 

# GLH must also follow the Copyright, Designs, and Patents Act 1988, which protects creative content such as images, videos, and text. To comply, the website will only use original media or licensed stock images, and content protection features will prevent users from downloading images. This affects the design of product pages, galleries, and marketing sections, ensuring the company’s creative content is secure and legally compliant.

# 

# The Consumer Rights Act 2015 (CRA) also applies to the GLH platform because customers are buying goods online. The website will provide accurate product descriptions, clear pricing, and up-to-date stock levels. Users will be able to track orders, request refunds, or report issues if products are faulty or misrepresented. The checkout process, product pages, and order history dashboards will include clear information about returns and refunds, ensuring customers are treated fairly and the system meets CRA requirements.

# 

# Because GLH sells local food and drink, food traceability and safety regulations must be followed. Each product page will show producer information, origin, and production methods, and the producer dashboard will include batch tracking and stock management. This ensures that orders are traceable from farm to customer and that the platform meets legal food safety requirements. The dashboard, product pages, and order management system are designed specifically to support these traceability features.

# 

# Finally, the platform will comply with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) to make the website usable for all customers, including those with disabilities. Features such as screen reader support, keyboard navigation, text resizing, high-contrast colour modes, and responsive layouts will be included. These accessibility features affect the design of menus, product listings, checkout forms, and dashboards, ensuring the platform is inclusive and provides a better experience for all users.
